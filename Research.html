Studies & Findings of PHYTOS

In Asia, young leaves, flowers, and pods of Sesbania grandiflora are commonly used in various culinary applications, including curries, soups, and salads. The tender pods are prepared like green beans, while the leaves and young pods enhance the flavor of dishes, especially in South Indian cuisine. The flowers are often incorporated into local recipes in countries such as Sri Lanka, Thailand, and the Philippines, showcasing their versatility and nutritional value.


Phytos, a natural supplement manufactured entirely in the Philippines, leverages the health benefits of these potent leaves. Extensive research indicates that Sesbania grandiflora provides essential vitamins, minerals, and natural compounds that protect cells, exhibit antioxidant properties, and offer antibacterial benefits, making it a valuable ally in diabetes management.

Physicochemical Analysis

Studies reveal the presence of functional groups such as alkaloids, saponins, and phenols in the leaves, contributing to its pharmacological properties.

Leaves have been analyzed for moisture, ash content, and extractive values, providing a comprehensive understanding of their chemical composition.

Health Benefits Related to Diabetes Management

Antidiabetic Activity

Antidiabetic Activity

Isolated alpha-glucosidase inhibitors, SGF60 and SGF90, have shown potential in regulating blood sugar levels.

Major metabolites from leaves and twigs exhibit significant inhibitory effects against alpha-glucosidase, a key enzyme in carbohydrate metabolism.

Antioxidant Properties

Antioxidant Properties

Methanolic extracts from Sesbania grandiflora possess high polyphenol content, providing substantial antioxidant activity which protects against oxidative stress and supports overall metabolic health.

Antibacterial Effects

Antibacterial Effects

Various extract fractions demonstrate significant antibacterial activity, particularly against Gram-negative bacteria, attributed to high levels of bioactive compounds such as flavonoids and tannins.

Hypolipidemic Effects

Hypolipidemic Effects

Studies indicate that Sesbania grandiflora can effectively lower serum cholesterol levels, improving lipid profiles and supporting cardiovascular health, which is crucial for diabetic patients.
